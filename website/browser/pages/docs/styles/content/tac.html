Content

Utilities for controlling how text content wraps, truncates, and clamps. Use single-line truncation with an ellipsis, or clamp text to a fixed number of lines.

Usage

Single-line truncation

Use text-truncate to clip a single line of text and append an ellipsis when it overflows.

This is a long single line of text that will be truncated with an ellipsis.

<p class="text-truncate">Single line, ellipsis</p>

Line clamping

Use w-line-clamp-1 through w-line-clamp-4 to limit text to a fixed number of lines. Use w-line-clamp-none to remove a clamp.

This paragraph is clamped to two lines maximum. Any text beyond the second line is hidden and replaced with an ellipsis at the end of the visible content.

This paragraph is clamped to three lines. Extra text beyond the third line is hidden and replaced with an ellipsis at the end of the visible content, keeping the layout compact.

<p class="w-line-clamp-2">Clamped to two lines</p> <p class="w-line-clamp-3">Clamped to three lines</p>

Text wrapping

Use the wrapping helpers to prevent wrapping, preserve whitespace, or force long words to break.

This text stays on one line even when the container is narrow.

Averylongwordthatbreaksotherwiseunbreakablecontent.

<p class="text-no-wrap">Stays on one line</p> <p class="w-text-break">Breakslongwords</p>

Reference
