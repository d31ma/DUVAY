Styles and animations

This section gathers the visual foundations: themes, typography, utilities, density, shape, elevation, and motion.

Style foundations

  • Themes define semantic tokens for color, spacing, shape, elevation, and high-contrast mode.
  • Animations documents transitions, expansion height, FLIP reorders, crossfades, tweened values, and spring motion.
  • Utilities cover spacing, display, flex, sizing, text, color, radius, and elevation helpers.

Motion principles

DuVay uses subtle transitions for hover, focus, overlays, drawers, expansion panels, and progress states. Motion must support comprehension, stay fast, and respect prefers-reduced-motion.
